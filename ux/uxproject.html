Tools Indesign, Photoshop, Illustrator, Justinmind, Invision, Gliffy

Length 9 weeks

Freshness, Health, Lifestyle, Conflict

In America, most of farmers sell their produce in two ways: supermarket and farmer’s market. Supermarket leads in the majority of sales, while farmer’s market accounts only 10% of it. However, the number is changing in recent years, farmer’s market share is increasing, because of the change in people’s lifestyle and concept of healthy living. More and more people want fresh seasonal food, local organic food, and a variety of produce. The weekly and distant farmer’s market can not satisfy a majority of people’s needs. So, the conflict between farmers and consumers are occurring.

Design Concept/Intent

Expose, Delivery, Convenience

FreshGrab intents to empower farmers by giving them the tools and means to expose their produce on the Internet; providing online ordering and shipping to customers or pick up by them; building statistics over farmers’ sales and keep track of their customers to easily stay connected with them. All the features are designed to meet farmers and customers’ needs and providing convenient to their life.

Design Process

Goals and objectives

On the main body of introduction, there are too much information and logos randomly showing up with no reason. The twitter and highway 1 logos are too big and can be put in a sidebar or on the footer. Also, content like “Things to do in Big Sur” “local weather” “highway conditions” can have their own navigation button on the top, so that it’s easier for people to look for.
In the lodging page, there are too many words about each place, making content and information closely packed and cluttered, don’t have enough white space and making people don’t feel relaxed. It’s not like what we could expect from a travel information website. And there are no map showing where they are and pictures about how they look like. It’s not easy for users to choose and locate one that is best for them and there is no “return to top” navigation in some pages.
In the contact page, the contact information is using red color and all cluttered together, which is hard to read. Some information like Big Sur Guide, map of California, together with the weather, highway conditions information should be together in another page, like “Useful information”.

Site Map

In order to reorganize the content and classify them, I divided the content into four navigations.

Logo & Type Font

The original website, to be more precisely, the organization(Big Sur Chamber of Commerce) doesn’t have a logo and the way he present himself on the website is vague and faded in the header image and not so unique, so I tried to use type font to make it unique and outstanding, to tell people where the website comes from.

The type font that I choose is called Herculanum. It gives me a feeling of breezy and rocky, which I think is perfectly for Big Sur. For the rest of the content and navigation, I tried to use only one type font but with several varieties to present a type font hierarchy.
