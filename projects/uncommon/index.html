Everything starts here

 In the beginning, when looking at Uncommon’s brief, we couldn’t figure out what was their pain points until we had our first client meeting with the CEO and product manager. The more we talked, the more we realized that their product is about to change the recruiting and hiring industry with their AI technology, However, in Uncommon’s database, there are lots of passive job seekers, and those people usually have a low rate of replying emails and take actions. Uncommon came to us hoping that we can investigate and design solutions to help to grow this market. In the meantime, to instigate a way to cut recruiter’s workload and be more efficient to find good matches. Hence, how to make a product more meaningful and efficient to both recruiters and job seekers?



Our task

 To engage qualified passive job seekers through Uncommon’s recruiter portal.

Design process model

We follow the double diamond design process, from divergent thinking to convergent thinking, with ideas and design tested and iterated cycling the entire process.

From the comparison, we found out that there are similar sourcing platforms existing for recruiters, but there is no focus on the job seeker side, especially for passive job seekers.

We highlighted some features that may potentially help us design for passive job seekers.


We also looked at some of our emails received from recruiters and recruiting company. We picked 3 of them representing different reasons that we refused to reply.

Synthesize & Define

Problem & Solution

Problem

Passive job seekers often ignore recruiter emails because they are impersonal, vague and require too much time and effort.

In order to motivate passive job seekers to explore and pursue new job opportunities, Uncommon needs to offer an experience that is personalized and user-friendly.

Solution

Create a customized experience for passive job seekers that inspires them to explore new job opportunities with Uncommon.

We achieve this by providing a simple and personalized applicant process that enables the user to quickly scan company details, qualify for the position, and schedule a time to discuss next steps, all in a matter of minutes.

Solution Design Features

  • Personalized & engaging email
  • Job description
  • Team information
  • Benefits
  • Brief screener survey
  • Appointment booking facility
  • Other relevant job postings

Design Principles

Tone

One of the key to motivate passive job seeker is the tone in the messages and through the product interface. People like caring, warm and human feeling messages. They want to feel trusted, feel like the recruiter really takes time to draft the email and the new company is really welcoming.

Content

Another important motivator to passive job seeker is the information provided: first in the email, and then through the application process. We will use progressive disclosure strategy to avoid information overload at a specific time. Also, the content needs to be personalized as much as possible.

Color

Uncommon already has a detailed style guide, I chose purple, blue and yellow to be the main color of the new product. Purple means trust, responsibility; yellow is the Uncommon's logo color, blue is its main tabs color.

Final Design

Job, Team, Benefits Sections
When users click on the link provided in the email, they will be redirected to Uncommon job seeker portal. On the top-right corner, it’s the recruiting company’s name.

There are detailed job information, team members self-introduction, working environment pictures, and benefits detail page.
Screener Questions & Appointment Booking
When users click Apply, they will enter application section. The header will change into yellow, but they can always go back and forth among job/team/benefits and application.

There will be 7 screener questions in total. As shown in our research, users will be more willing to stay when there are less than 8 questions.

When users pass the screener, they can choose to continue to book an appointment. By default, the “yes” option is selected and the calendar appears to encourage users to continue the process. This “tab”calendar only shows a few options, which reduces users decision making time, hence reduces the risk of quitting. For the same reason, I also put the “No”option down to the page to make it less obvious.
Negative Choice & Result
If users haven’t made up their mind yet, they can select "No" option. Then, there will be two options appearing: remind about this job; notify about similar jobs. If users select “Remind”, then 3 dates tabs will appear to save users time of thinking and there will be a confirmation pop-up window.

If users fail the screener, they can still choose to be notified about similar jobs, upload resume and check out other recommendations.
