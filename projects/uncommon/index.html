Reinventing the recruiting process through AI technology

Project Overview

Uncommon connects job seekers and employers through the use of data and intelligence. This project aims to open its business to passive job seekers by providing a responsive web app that removes obstacles that stifle the employment process, that motivates them to move forward, and provides tools for recruiters to pre-screen and find talent more efficiently and accurately.

How it started

 In the beginning, when looking at Uncommon’s brief, we couldn’t figure out what was their pain points until we had our first client meeting with the CEO and product manager. The more we talked, the more it became clear that their product was about to change the recruiting and human resource industry with their AI technology. However, Uncommon’s database contains a large number of passive job seekers, and those people usually have a low rate of replying emails and take actions. Uncommon came to us hoping that we could investigate and design a solution to help grow this market, as well as instigate a way to cut recruiters' workload and be more efficient to find good matches. Hence our challenge, how to make a product more meaningful and efficient to both recruiters and job seekers?

Research & Discover

Competitive analysis

We are new to this industry and the technology involved. In order to understand more about the market, we did some research to find out who were the competitors and what they were doing.

From the comparison, we found out that there were similar sourcing platforms existing for recruiters, but there was no focus on the job seeker side, especially for passive job seekers.

We highlighted some features that may potentially help us design for passive job seekers.

User survey & interviews As there was no such product existing, we designed surveys and conducted interviews aimed at recruiters and job seekers in the U.S. to learn about their needs.

Recruiter Interviews We got introduced to recruiters through Uncommon and our networks and friends. We also interviewed 3 recruiters who were in the same building as us.

  • 9 recruiters
  • Some questions we asked:
    • Where do you look for talents?
    • What is your interview process?
    • How many sourced candidates reply?
    • What reasons do they give when not interested?

Main Findings

  • Most used recruiting source: LinkedIn
  • Respond with interest: 2-4% ONLY
  • Strongest reason for lack of interest:
    • Happy with current job
    • Timing (waiting for promotion)
    • Not interested in company

Job Seeker Survey We designed a survey on Google Forms and received 19 responses. Most people are somewhat happy with their job, but often not fully due to various reasons, and they were open to opportunities that may be better than what they had.

Passive Job Seekers Interviews After the survey, we conducted 9 interviews to seek deeper insights beyond the survey answers.

  • Some questions we asked:
    • Do you ever hear from recruiters?
    • When you receive emails from recruiters, what do you do?
    • What is your impression of the emails?
    • Is there any way that an email from a recruiter could get your interest?
    • What are you unhappy with at your job?
    • When is the last time you posted a résumé online?
  • People were saying:

    • “At least I know the challenges of my current job.”

    • “I delete recruiter emails and don’t read them because they are unsolicited.”

    • “I want to work in a positive work environment. Devoid of negativity and negative people. And that starts with management.”

Problem & Solution

Problem

Passive job seekers often ignore recruiter emails because they are impersonal, vague and require too much time and effort.

In order to motivate passive job seekers to explore and pursue new job opportunities, Uncommon needs to offer an experience that is personalized and user-friendly.

Solution

Create a customized and motivating experience for passive job seekers that lead them to change jobs.

We achieve this by providing a simple and personalized process which enables the user to quickly scan company details, pass job qualification screeners, and schedule a time to discuss next steps.

Solution Design Features

  • Personalized & engaging email
  • Job description
  • Team information
  • Benefits
  • Brief screener survey
  • Appointment booking facility
  • Other relevant job postings

Design Principles

Tone

One of the key to motivate passive job seekers is the tone in the messages and through the product interface. People like caring, warm and human feeling messages. They want to feel trusted, feel like the recruiter really took time to draft his email, and that the company is really worth joining.

Content

Another important motivator to passive job seekers is the information provided: first in the email, and then through the application process. We will use the progressive disclosure strategy to avoid information overload at a specific time. Futhermore, the content needs to be personalized as much as possible.

Color

Uncommon already has a detailed style guide. I chose purple, blue and yellow to be the main color of the new product. Purple means trust, responsibility; yellow is Uncommon's logo color, blue is its secondary and complementary color.

Design & Deliver

Wireframes & Hi-Fi Design I designed 6 initial conceptual wireframes for the major sections, including: job/team/benefits information, screener questions, results confirmation page, after which I developed them into full visual compositions following our design principles.


Usability testing & iterations We asked 4 people to do usability testing on the full comp design. We tracked their feedback and consolidated it into iterations.

  • Sometimes users don’t see the button when they scroll the page.
  • The names/tab on the header would confuse users about where they are.
  • The radio button may be hard to click on a phone.
  • The progress bar indicates which question users were on, but the way to show it is slighlty unclear.
  • The traditional calendar is hard to read and tap on a mobile device.
  • The options under “No, not yet” section are complicated for users to choose. They might just leave the page. Need to simplify the choices to lessen their effort.
  • Recruiters already have users emails, and adding a separate page for their phone number increases clicks.

Confirmation Messages Iterations For people who pass the screener questions, we need to motivate them to move to the next step by expressing a concise and clear message, but saying "a great match" is confusing candidates. For people who don’t pass, the message needs to deliver a clear hint, but in a comfortable, encouraging way.

Final Design

Job, Team, Benefits Sections
When users click on the link provided in the email, they will be redirected to Uncommon job seeker portal. On the top-right corner, it’s the recruiting company’s name.

There are detailed job information, team members self-introductions, work environment pictures, and benefits detail page.
Screener Questions & Appointment Booking
When users click Apply, they will enter the application section. The header will change into yellow, but they can always go back and forth among job/team/benefits and application.

There will be 7 screener questions in total. As shown in our research, users will be more willing to stay when there are less than 8 questions.

When users pass the screener, they can choose to continue to book an appointment. By default, the “yes” option is selected and the calendar appears to encourage users to continue the process. This “calendar tab” only shows a few options, which reduces users decision making time, hence reduces the risk of quitting. For the same reason, I also put the “No” option down to the page to make it less obvious.
Negative Choice & Result
If users haven’t made up their mind yet, they can select "No" option. Then, there will be two options appearing: remind about this job; notify about similar jobs. If users select “Remind”, then 3 suggested dates will appear to save users time and there will be a confirmation pop-up window.

If users fail the screener, they can still choose to be notified about similar jobs, upload their résumé, and check out other recommendations.
